Global Studies

My name is Sofia Ochoa, I'm a first year at the University of California Riverside and I am a Global Studies major. My focus is on learning about world politics and how different cultures interact with each other.

Most of my experience comes from working in youth politics across the city. I was the Vice Chair of the mayor's youth council and established a deep connection with my city's roots through volunteering and working in local politics. My experience with local politics has led me to develop a curiosity for the inner funtionings of political structures across the world. I've been involved in several projects across the city focusing on youth involvement in local politics such as the Riverside Youth Summit, Mock City Council meeting, Martin Luther King Jr. Walk, Love Riverside, and so much more. I love working with people and learning more about others' backgrounds. I've grown my love for communication through my day to day interactions and my own experiences growing up in a interracial household. My fascination with global studies stems from my need to understand the history of the cultures that make up me. History and foreign language are subjects that'll never cease to fascinate me.

Besides my experience in politics, I have a deep passion for the arts and culture. I've been involved in the arts ever since I could perform. Being able to express my creativity is a integral part of who I am. I'd love to delve into how art is reflected within different cultures in my future career. Music, acting, and writing are passions that I will never grow out of. I've been a part of choir for five years and have been singing even longer than that. I've been writing ever since I learned to, poetry and songwriting are my strong suits and main interests. Acting is something I'd appreciate doing more of, I've performed in several shows and am excited to do many more. Art is immortal and integral to the development of society as any form of new technology. Through art we are able to express personal experiences and form deep connections with others.It is an absolutely necessary part of who we are as human beings.
